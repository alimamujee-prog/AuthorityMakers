**Every word of your marketing should be written for Marcus.**

Authority Makers was built specifically for that. Two hours a month in front of a camera. Everything else handled. The firms that started this 12 months ago are not watching their competitor's YouTube channel with concern anymore. They became the channel their competitors are watching. Your market might still be available. Let us find out.

### Brand Voice & Tone

- **Specific, authoritative, plain-spoken.** Write like a strategist, not a marketer.
- Maintain at least a **2:1 ratio of "you/your" to "we/our"** in all copy.
- **One CTA only:** "See if your market is available." Never compete with secondary CTAs.
- **Banned words:** "solution," "leverage," "robust," "seamless."
- **Specificity is credibility:** Real numbers, timeframes, firm names, and market names. No anonymous testimonials or vague praise.
- **Problem before solution:** Let the problem land before offering relief. The villain is invisibility, not a competitor.

## 3. Homepage Best Practices

**https://authority-makers.vercel.app/index.html**

### The 7-Section Framework

The homepage follows a proven conversion framework. Each section has a specific job:

| # | Section | Background | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Navigation** | Dark | Logo left, nav center (3-4 links max), CTA button right. Button says the action ("Book a Call"), not the company name |
| 2 | **Hero** | Dark | Headline (8-12 words max), subheadline, single CTA, visual element. Lead with outcome or problem, not company description |
| 3 | **Social Proof Bar** | Light | Client logos OR one high-credibility stat. Recognition moment, not a reading moment. Sits between hero and explanation |
| 4 | **Problem** | Dark | Name the current situation, the limitation, and the cost of inaction. Use the client's exact language. Let the problem land before offering relief |
| 5 | **Solution / How It Works** | Light | Maximum 3 steps. Lead with capabilities, not features. Each step answers: "What does my life look like after this?" |
| 6 | **Proof** | Dark | Named case studies (strongest), pull quotes with full attribution, results metrics. Start with doubt, end with transformation |
| 7 | **Final CTA** | Dark | One headline, one supporting line, one button. Urgency must be real. Resolution, not desperation |

### Section Background Rhythm

Dark → Dark → Light → Dark → Light → Dark → Dark

This alternation creates visual breathing room and guides the eye down the page.

### Universal Copy Rules

1. **Clarity over cleverness.** If it sounds like marketing, rewrite it.
2. **One ICP, written for with uncomfortable specificity.** Broad appeal beats nobody.
3. **Features are table stakes, not differentiators.** Lead with the mechanism — the 2-hour batch system, consult attribution, one firm per market.
4. **Every line must earn the next line.** If a sentence doesn't increase desire or reduce friction, cut it.

### Scroll-Driven Video (Technical Notes)

The hero uses a scroll-mapped video animation — the video plays forward as the user scrolls down:

- **Format:** MP4, 1920x1080, 8-15 seconds duration
- **Setup:** `<video muted playsinline preload="auto">` — never autoplay or controls
- **Scroll mapping:** `window.scrollY` maps to `video.currentTime` via `requestAnimationFrame`
- **Scroll zone:** 400vh (4x viewport height) for smooth, granular scrubbing
- **Text overlays:** `position: fixed` with scroll-fraction-based opacity/scale
- **Performance:** `will-change: transform, opacity` on animated elements; adaptive lerp interpolation for buttery smooth playback
- **Mobile:** `playsinline` (required for iOS Safari), `-webkit-overflow-scrolling: touch`

The full technical specification is included in the zip: `strategy/scroll-animation-best-practices.md`
